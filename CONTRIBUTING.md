# 🤝 Contributing to The Journey of Wisdom (Seir-e Hekmat)

Thank you for your interest in contributing to **The Journey of Wisdom**! This open-source academic initiative aims to map the continuous history, transmission, and critique of global wisdom traditions.

We welcome contributions from researchers, historians, philologists, translators, and open-knowledge advocates worldwide.

---

## 🧭 Ways to Contribute

You can participate in several areas:

1. **Academic & Textual Contributions (`/volumes`)**
   * Reviewing, refining, or expanding draft chapters for Volume I, II, or III.
   * Providing cross-cultural translations or critical commentary.

2. **Research & Citation (`/research`)**
   * Suggesting peer-reviewed sources, critical editions, or primary manuscripts.
   * Correcting historical, epigraphic, or chronological inaccuracies.

3. **White Sheets Data (`/white-sheets`)**
   * Submitting new archival, epigraphic, or archaeological entries spanning ancient hubs (e.g., Bactria/Balkh) to 18th-century Europe.
   * Updating structural data using the **5-Folder Model**.

---

## 🔬 Research Standards & Methodology

To maintain academic integrity, all contributions must adhere to the **5-Folder Model**:

* **Persons:** Lineages, historical agents, and biographies must be corroborated by reliable sources.
* **Texts:** Direct references to manuscripts, colophons, or published critical editions are required.
* **Institutions:** Historical academies, guilds, and circles should be documented with spatial and temporal context.
* **Concepts:** Philosophical vocabulary should preserve multi-lingual terminology (e.g., Greek, Persian, Arabic, Latin).
* **Symbols:** Structural, geometric, and ritualistic analysis must avoid purely speculative claims without historical context.

---

## 🛠️ How to Submit Your Contribution

1. **Fork the Repository:** Create your own copy of the repository.
2. **Create a Branch:** Name your branch clearly (e.g., `fix/balkh-date-correction` or `feature/vol1-ch3-translation`).
3. **Commit Your Changes:** Keep your commit messages clear and descriptive.
4. **Open a Pull Request (PR):** Describe your proposed changes, citing primary or secondary sources where applicable.

---

## 💬 Issues & Discussions

* **Bug Reports & Errors:** If you spot a factual error, broken reference, or typo, please open an **Issue**.
* **Academic Debate:** Use the **Discussions** tab to propose new theoretical frameworks or discuss controversial historical nodes.
